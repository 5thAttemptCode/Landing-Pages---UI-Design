North American Grizzly

Grizzly Bear

The grizzly bear (Ursus arctos horribilis), also known as the North American brown bear or simply grizzly, is a population or subspecies of the brown bear inhabiting North America.In addition to the mainland grizzly, other morphological forms of brown bear in North America are sometimes identified as grizzly bears. These include two living populations — the Kodiak bear and the peninsular grizzly — as well as the extinct California grizzly, Mexican grizzly, and Ungava-Labrador grizzly.
